Social Interactions



Summary

- Set the tone first
- Move slowly and comfortably
- Smile and greet everyone
- Be cool, have a "I don’t care" attitude

- One up the energy level in the room
- Lighten the mood and have fun
- Be more enthusiastic, energetic, positive
- Don’t try to be "normal", be yourself


5 Tipps how to act in social settings

(1) Move slow and don't rush when speaking - give a care free attitude
- Determine mood I want for interaction (playful, flirty, funny)
- spend first 20 to 30 seconds setting that

(2) Greet everyone around you
- Many make the mistake of only greeting the main person (that makes it seem like you want something from them)
- For a deeper connection, combine this with a touch, hug or handshake

(3) Be comfortable in every environment and exude that comfort and relaxated mood
- Take more space than usual
- Open your body language
- Physical comfort translates to mental comfort putting you at ease

(4) Stay anchored to yourself
- Don't do what others are doing
- Stay true to yourself, even if everyone else is different
- Choose who you want to be and be that (don't change to blend in with others around you)

(5) Be a magnetic story teller
- Use anything, the key is a good pre frame
- Hook them i.e. So here's a good story...
- Take them on an emotional journey, share your emotional journey
- Act out the characters
- Use speaking cadence and posture
- Tell the story with your hands
- Tell the story in the present tense

Other tipps

Keeping conversations going

(1) Say: This reminds me of... (and then choose something)
(2) Ask open ended questions (3) Ask "Why" or "how" and what they were feeling

Revival Questions

(1) How do you know one another?
(2) What's your story?
(3) What's your plan for the future?
(4) What keeps you awake at night / most interested in at the moment?
(5) What is the best thing that has happended to you all day / week / month / year / ever?

Super Power Question

(1) If you could have any superpower, what would you choose?
(2) What would you then do with that power?
(3) How would that make you feel if you had that?

- Listen carefully
- Ask questions to draw out values, dreams, fears and aspirations
- Mirror, explain and disect

Complimentary Cold Read

- Observe someone (what they wear, look like, say, etc)
- Make a compliment based on that i.e. You look like you're into fitness

Complimentary and Joke

- Give someone a compliment
- and add a "harmless" joke

Joke answer

- Answer a normal question with a joke, i.e. take the real answer and exaggerate it extremely
- Make sure to not do that too often or too extreme as it can be annoying
